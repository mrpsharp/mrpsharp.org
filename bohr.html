In 1913, Bohr used the criteria that the angular momentum of the electron is quantised:

$$ mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi} $$

This was later reinterpreted by de Broglie as a standing wave condition: the electron acts as a wave and a whole number of wavelengths fits along the circumference: $$ n\lambda = 2\pi r $$ Therefore the Kinetic Energy can be calculated using:

$$ E_k = \frac{p^2}{2m_e} = \frac{h^2n^2}{2\pi r}$$

The potential energy of the electron is given by:

$$ E_p = -\frac{Ze^2}{4\pi\epsilon_0 r}$$
